## Commercial Viability Factors for Nature Photography

### High Commercial Value Indicators (8-10):
- Iconic or instantly recognizable locations
- Perfect weather conditions (dramatic skies, golden hour, fog)
- Wildlife in action or expressing emotion
- Seasonal peak moments (fall colors, spring blooms)
- Clean, uncluttered compositions
- Universal appeal subjects (sunsets, rainbows, baby animals)
- Strong emotional triggers (awe, peace, wonder)
- Suitable for large format printing
- Versatile for multiple uses (wall art, calendars, cards)
- Trending subjects on social media

### Medium Commercial Value (5-7):
- Good technical quality but common subjects
- Regional interest rather than universal
- Specific niche markets (birders, hikers, locals)
- Documentary value over artistic merit
- Good stock photography potential
- Suitable for editorial use
- Environmental storytelling
- Educational content potential

### Lower Commercial Value (1-4):
- Over-photographed locations without unique perspective
- Technical flaws affecting print quality
- Cluttered or confusing compositions
- Limited emotional engagement
- Niche scientific or documentary interest only
- Poor lighting or weather conditions
- Subjects requiring extensive context to appreciate
- Limited print size potential due to quality

### Price Tier Guidelines:
- HIGH ($500+): Gallery quality, unique perspectives of iconic subjects, museum-worthy prints
- MID ($150-500): Strong commercial appeal, excellent technical quality, broad market interest
- LOW ($50-150): Good quality, niche appeal, smaller print potential, beginner collector market
